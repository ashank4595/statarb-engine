Say on 1st Jan the market closes at 400 INR at 3pm (market close time), and this spread is high — the z-spread crossed the red line. So I have to wait until the market next reopens, 2nd Jan 9am, and then I short the spread at that time (the daily-bar model approximates this using the next day's close). Then if on Tuesday I am lucky and the z-spread goes back into the grey zone to 300 at 3pm on Tuesday, I have earned 100, excluding transaction costs. This 100 will be the PnL put on Tuesday in the results df gross_pnl for 2nd Jan. Although this profit is realized on Tuesday, I only used Monday's data for it. If we did not shift positions, then the -1 for shorting would be on Monday, so I would short Monday's price while using Monday's close, which is impossible — and I would actually lose money, as by 3pm I saw the price rise above the red line to 400.
